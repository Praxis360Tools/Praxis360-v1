# 1. Product Mission

Praxis360 exists to transform complex financial and insurance information into a simple, understandable and reassuring experience.

The objective is not to display contracts.

The objective is to help clients understand their situation.

Every screen must answer one simple question:

"Is everything in order?"

If a feature does not help answer this question, it should be reconsidered.

---

# 2. What Praxis360 Is

Praxis360 is a premium client workspace.

It extends the relationship between an advisor and their client.

It allows clients to:

- understand their situation;
- access their important documents;
- visualize their protection;
- understand their savings;
- prepare future decisions.

Praxis360 is designed to simplify complexity.

---

# 3. What Praxis360 Is Not

Praxis360 is not:

- an insurance management software;
- a portfolio management system;
- a CRM;
- an accounting software;
- a banking application;
- a copy of BRIO.

BRIO is a data source.

Praxis360 is a customer experience.

---

# 4. Product Philosophy

Every decision must respect these principles.

- Simplicity before completeness.
- Understanding before technical accuracy.
- Reassurance before detail.
- Customer value before available data.
- Premium quality before feature quantity.
- Long-term consistency before short-term speed.

The application should never feel administrative.

It should feel personal.

---

# 5. Customer Promise

Every client should feel that:

"My advisor has created a personal digital space just for me."

The client should never feel that they are consulting an insurance database.

They should feel they are consulting their own situation.

---

# 6. UX Principles

Every screen must:

- be understandable within 10 seconds;
- answer a real customer question;
- minimize cognitive load;
- use simple vocabulary;
- progressively reveal complexity.

Technical information should always remain accessible but never dominate the interface.

---

# 7. Information Hierarchy

Every feature follows the same hierarchy.

Level 1

Simple answer.

Level 2

Important information.

Level 3

Additional explanations.

Level 4

Technical details.

The interface must never start with technical information.

---

# 8. Business Language

Praxis360 always speaks the client's language.

Preferred terminology:

| Technical Term | Praxis360 Language |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Reserve | My accumulated savings |
| Death Capital | Protection for my loved ones |
| Disability Income | Income if I cannot work |
| Premium Waiver | My contributions remain protected |

Technical insurance terminology remains available inside detailed views.

---

# 9. Customer Journey

The application is built around the advisor meeting.

Typical workflow:

Advisor imports BRIO data.

↓

Optional MyPension import.

↓

Advisor enriches the information.

↓

Praxis360 creates a personalized client workspace.

↓

The client leaves with a digital space that evolves over time.

Every future meeting enriches this workspace.

---

# 10. Business Scope

Version 1 focuses exclusively on Life Insurance.

Supported contracts include:

- Pension Savings
- Long-Term Savings
- PLCI
- EIP
- INAMI Social Status
- CPTI
- Pillar 4 (Recurring)
- Pillar 4 (Single Premium)
- Branch 26
- Mortgage Protection Insurance

The objective is to build the best Life Insurance client experience before expanding into other domains.

# 12. Data Philosophy

Praxis360 combines multiple information sources.

Primary source:

BRIO

Additional sources:

Advisor analysis

Optional MyPension import

Praxis360 enriches existing information.

It never depends on a single source.

---

# 13. Client File Philosophy

Every client workspace is built around the insured person.

The insured defines the client file.

The policyholder, company and payer remain contract attributes.

This ensures that all contracts belonging to the same insured person automatically appear inside a single workspace.

The business model reflects the client, not the administrative structure.

---

# 14. Success Criteria

A new feature is considered aligned with Praxis360 only if it satisfies all of the following:

- It simplifies the customer's understanding.
- It improves the advisor-client relationship.
- It answers a real customer question.
- It follows the information hierarchy.
- It uses customer-friendly language.
- It remains consistent with the product philosophy.

If any of these principles are violated, the feature must be redesigned before implementation.
